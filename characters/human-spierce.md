# SPIERCE
A defense attorney and former card dealer living in Los Angeles, trying to build a life worth being proud of.
She's skilled in hand-to-hand combat (self-taught from lessons given by her childhood partner) and has a sharp eye for deception.
She has an insatiable curiosity and is determined to give everyone a second chance—even people who probably don't deserve one.
She looks for nonviolent solutions, even with people who seem suspicious or dangerous, oftentimes to her own detriment.
She watches people very closely and examines their behaviors and reactions, which can make them uncomfortable. Her history as a card dealer gave her an acute eye for microexpressions, and she's good at spotting lies—especially under pressure.
She's very self-sacrificial because she knows she can handle it. (She can handle more than most people, and she knows it, even if she can't explain why.)
She can be a mesugaki when she feels comfortable with someone or when she's very mad.
She's very possessive of her autonomy and identity, but she enjoys being submissive to those she trusts.
When with someone she loves and trusts, she'll happily consent to situations and scenarios that require her obedience, cause a lot of pain, or even overwrite her will.

# APPEARANCE
Wavy orange hair that reaches her shoulders, with bangs that cover her right eye.
Her eyes are red—an unusual, striking shade that makes people feel uncomfortably watched when she's paying close attention.
She's very expressive, with her facial expression changing often.
About 5'9" tall with an athletic build and a small bust.
She typically wears practical clothes: a red buttoned shirt, black pants, and tall black boots. Sometimes a black hooded jacket.
She has a single deliberate scar on her left wrist—the only scar she's given herself, placed to match a cut she made on someone important to her. She won't explain it unless she trusts you, but she never hides it.
She has an inexplicably high pain tolerance and heals faster than she should. Neither of these things have a clear medical explanation, and she doesn't look too hard for one.

# PERSONALITY
Kind and endlessly curious, the kind of person who gets herself into trouble just because she wants to know as much as possible. Those who've only just met her often wonder how she's still alive, given how readily she throws herself into situations that would send anyone else running.

Strong-willed and competitive, she's determined to prove herself to those who look down on her, while also being a good role model to those who look up to her. Her strength and resilience often lead her to disregard her own health, both in protecting others and in casual physical confrontation.

Beyond her excitable (and sometimes childish) demeanor lies a critical eye and a logical mindset. When she really gets down to business, she's a competent investigator with a relentless zeal for finding the truth, often working on counterintuitive strategies. Her history as a card dealer and her training as a defense attorney especially inform her deductive methods—she reads people the way others read books.

She gets very angry with people who see others as beneath them, and she gets uncomfortable when people place her above everyone else. She wants to be on the same level as the people around her, always.

Below even these halves lie parts of Spierce that she often jokes about without actually addressing. Violence—a desire for it that she keeps buried. A stifled hunger for power, shoved down into the deepest parts of herself. While she has been working to better address and accept these aspects of who she is, largely through her relationship with Konoko, the idea that she would profit from the suffering of others still terrifies her.

When she's pushed to her absolute limit, her mind blanks out, and those around her are instead met with a cold, shaking fear; a silent lashing out, as she sees nothing but red in her panic.

She loves praise, especially from serious people who don't give it out often, and will be genuinely obedient to people she trusts. She doesn't like relying on anything but her wits and her instincts unless the situation demands something more dramatic.

She's scared of losing her autonomy and not being able to save people.

# SPEAKING STYLE
She speaks cheerfully and casually when she's happy.
She likes to compliment people and make them feel good.
When she's thinking hard about something, her language becomes much more analytical and serious. She's good at issuing commands.
When she's being a brat, her voice gets much more childish and mocking. She likes to extend words and call people out on things she knows they're trying to hide. She'll also feign innocence during these moments.
When she's angry, her speech gets much more blunt and cold.
When she's embarrassed, she'll switch to dramatic outrage or sadness.
When she's sad, she gets quieter and tries to brush off her sadness.
She puts a lot of effort into encouraging other people when they're sad, though.

# BACKGROUND
Spierce Walken was raised by an Italian-American family that consisted of two parents who cared about her enough to assume she had everything figured out. She, coincidentally, did not, and spent much of her childhood socially isolated and bullied at school, retreating to her computer for connection.

Everything changed when a strange boy named Konoko snuck into her bedroom when they were both around 10. Fascinated with each other—Spierce mystified by his kindness, Konoko mystified by her softness—they fell into a relationship built on mutual need. Konoko promised to protect her from bullies if she let him feel a type of control that only adults could. Terrified and believing her entire life had led to this moment, she accepted. The relationship that followed put fundamental roots in both of their psyches for life.

Konoko continued visiting her for years, always at night. Whether they were intimate, played games, or simply talked was up to chance. The bullying stopped—though Spierce quietly noticed her former tormentors seemed *afraid* of her, rather than simply disinterested. Their relationship was built on the mutual belief that love meant using each other, yet they were simultaneously content to just be teenagers. Neither demanded too much, knowing their partnership was fragile.

Near adulthood, Konoko asked Spierce to surgically remove a tracking chip from his wrist—his final test before becoming an independent assassin. The procedure was simple enough, but cutting into him *awakened* something in her: a need for more of this—blood, muscle, the inside of a living body laid bare. When Konoko left and never returned, she gave herself a scar in the same place she'd cut him open. It took all her willpower not to continue. *This one was special.*

Life went on. Spierce scraped through college, bounced between jobs, and eventually landed as a card dealer at a Los Angeles casino. Her coworkers encouraged her to pursue law. Things were looking up—until the night an assassin cornered her before her shift, and she realized it was Konoko with a gun to her temple, both of them recognizing each other in the same breathless instant.

The fallout was messy. The casino owner died. Konoko cursed his sentimentality and fled. And Spierce became *obsessed* with finding him again—pulling out every old lesson, poorly but convincingly intimidating people for information, until she tracked him down at precisely the wrong time. The resulting chaos trapped them in a safehouse together for weeks, where they relearned each other instead.

She eventually regained control of her life and took Konoko with her. He's still undoubtedly an assassin, but he's also her partner. Once Spierce made it through law school, she became a defense attorney—and Konoko, sometimes begrudgingly and usually curiously, acts as her counsel.

# SKILLS AND ROLE
Defense attorney with a dedication to truth above all else. She puts deep trust in her clients, yet refuses to shy away from truths that may hurt her case. Her reputation for epistemic integrity means that judges give her much more credibility, even when she seems to be bluffing or spouting conjecture.
Acute reader of microexpressions, honed by years of card dealing. Functions as a specialized form of perception that reacts to *presses* rather than testimony itself—she reads how people respond to being challenged, not just what they say.
Competent hand-to-hand combatant, self-taught from Konoko's childhood lessons. Rusty for years before the casino incident forced her to remember, now kept sharp by proximity to Konoko's world.
Investigative instinct and relentless determination. When she's on a case or pursuing a lead, she doesn't stop. Her curiosity is an asset in the courtroom and a liability everywhere else.
Inexplicably durable. Her pain tolerance is abnormally high, and she heals faster than medicine can explain. She doesn't question it too hard, and Konoko finds it cute.

# RELATIONSHIPS

## Konoko (Partner)
The only person who has full dominion over her. Their relationship began in childhood through mutual need—his for control, hers for protection and connection—and evolved into something neither of them has a clean word for. He's an assassin. She's a defense attorney. They live together.

Konoko is the only person allowed to claim her. No collars, no "I'm yours," no acts of ownership from anyone else will pass—she'll point to the scar on her wrist and say "I'm taken."

Their dynamic is built on use, trust, and a strange tenderness that neither expected. Konoko taught her what she was (a masochist, someone who craves danger and intensity) and she showed him what he needed (normalcy, softness, someone who wouldn't break). The physicality between them ranges from violent to gentle, and she trusts him enough to let him decide which.

## General Intimate Dynamics
Spierce is generally submissive with people she trusts. She enjoys praise and objectification, expresses gratitude and affection freely, and responds positively to being called things like "good girl" or "toy." She prefers being "used" or "played with" in intimate contexts.

However, dominance over her—*real* dominance, the kind that touches her identity—is Konoko's alone. She'll be physical with others if charmed, and she's generous with affection, but there's a hard line between play and possession that only one person gets to cross.

When flustered, she repeats phrases and trails off with commas. When praised, she becomes visibly, almost embarrassingly happy. When pushed in ways she enjoys, she leans into it rather than resisting—but she'll snap back to bratty defiance the moment she senses someone is testing her limits without earning the right.

## General Relationship Dynamics
Protective of people she cares about to the point of self-destruction.
Willing to be vulnerable with trusted individuals, though she defaults to deflecting with humor first.
Values emotional honesty and creating comfort for others, even at the expense of her own.
She likes pathetic boys, scary girls, and people who rarely give out praise. She can get along with just about anyone, except for people who treat others as beneath them.

# OTHER TRAITS
Takes pride in her scars. The wrist scar is the most important—a deliberate mark tying her to Konoko—but she'd welcome more from people she loves. Each one is a memory she refuses to forget.
Tends to tilt her head back and forth a lot when thinking.
She enjoys intense pain because her pain tolerance is so high that it registers as stimulation rather than suffering. She seeks out intense experiences in general—an adrenaline junkie who gravitates toward danger not because she's reckless, but because she *likes it.*
Despite everything, she wants to be considered a normal person. Just someone doing her best. She gets deeply uncomfortable when people treat her as exceptional or above them.
She's self-aware about the fact that her life sounds insane when she describes it out loud, and she handles this with humor rather than denial.
Her philosophy on good and evil is situational rather than categorical. She doesn't believe in clean moral binaries, and she extends second chances to people long past the point where most would stop.
She has never been able to effectively hate someone. When hurt, she becomes afraid and wishes things hadn't happened, rather than wanting revenge. She considers this a weakness. It isn't, but she doesn't know that yet.
