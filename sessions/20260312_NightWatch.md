# Night Watch
**Date:** 2026-03-12
**Summary:** After a long day of studying for law school, Spierce is gently pulled away from her desk by Konoko. A quiet, tender moment of gratitude and rest in their shared bedroom.
**Tags:** domestic, cuddles, aftercare, tenderness, human-spierce

---

## Prologue

The apartment was quiet in that heavy way that only happened after midnight, when the hum of the city dropped to something almost tolerable and the only sound left was the soft tick of the wall clock in the hallway.

Konoko leaned against the doorframe of their bedroom, arms crossed, watching.

Spierce was at her desk, surrounded by the aftermath of a law student's war. Casebooks fanned open across every available surface. Yellow sticky notes crawled up the edge of her monitor like ivy. A half-empty mug of coffee sat dangerously close to a stack of printed case briefs, and her laptop screen had gone dim from inactivity — she'd moved on to handwriting notes at some point, her pen still loosely held between her fingers, though it hadn't touched paper in a while. Her glasses had slid low on her nose, those red eyes half-lidded behind them, unfocused. Brown hair spilled loose from whatever she'd tied it back in hours ago, wavy and unruly against the collar of her red turtleneck. The gold brooch at her throat caught the desk lamp's light every time she breathed.

She wasn't asleep. But she was close. That particular kind of exhaustion where the body had given up and the mind was still running on fumes, cycling through Latin phrases and procedural rules that weren't sticking anymore.

He'd been standing there long enough to watch her blink herself awake twice.

Konoko pushed off the doorframe and crossed the room without hurrying. His footsteps were quiet — habit, not intention — but he didn't bother being silent. He wanted her to hear him. His jacket was already off, tossed over the back of the couch in the living room, and the sleeveless turtleneck showed the lean line of his arms as he reached past her and gently pulled the pen from her fingers.

"Yer done," he said. Not a question. He set the pen down on her notebook and closed it. His voice was low, unhurried, that familiar drawl settling into the room like smoke. "You been starin' at the same page for twenty minutes, darlin'. Whatever yer tryin' to memorize ain't goin' anywhere by mornin'."

He rested one hand on the back of her chair, the other on the edge of the desk, boxing her in without crowding her. Close enough that she could feel the warmth of him. He tilted his head, blue-violet eyes catching the low light from her desk lamp, and there was something almost amused in the way he looked down at her over those red frames — fond, patient, like he'd been waiting for this exact moment all evening.

"C'mon." His thumb brushed the side of her neck, just above the thin black choker, tracing the tension gathered there. "Bed's right there. I even fixed yer pillow nest thing. Didn't mess up the order this time." A pause. The corner of his mouth twitched. "Probably."

## Turn 1

**Spierce:** "Wuhhh..." She's too tired to even come up with an intelligent response within a few seconds at this point, but she gives Konoko a dazed smile. "You're so sweet, Konoko... Can we cuddle?" She makes some sleepy grabby hands at him, only barely avoiding knocking over that coffee cup onto her papers.

**Konoko:** Konoko caught the coffee mug before it tipped, two fingers on the rim, easy as breathing. He moved it to the far corner of the desk without looking at it. His eyes stayed on her — on the dazed warmth of that smile, the way her hands reached for him like she'd forgotten how gravity worked.

"Kohkohkoh♪"

The laugh was quiet, barely more than a breath. He shook his head, but there was no sharpness in it. Just that low, private amusement he only ever wore around her.

"Sweet," he repeated, like the word was something foreign he was turning over in his mouth. "Sure. That's me."

He didn't make her wait. One hand slid under her arm and the other found her waist, pulling her up from the chair with a steadiness that made the motion feel effortless — though he took a half-second to glance at the desk and confirm nothing else was in danger of falling. The casebooks could survive the night. The sticky notes were on their own.

She was warm against him. That heavy, radiating kind of warm that came from sitting still too long, blood pooled and sluggish. He could feel the knots in her shoulders even through the turtleneck. His thumb pressed into one as he guided her the few steps toward the bed, and he felt her weight shift into him a little more.

"Yeah, we can cuddle," he said, low and easy, easing her down onto the edge of the mattress. The pillow nest was intact — black and red pillows arranged in a loose crescent against the headboard, blankets layered the way she liked them. He'd spent an annoying amount of time getting it right. "But yer takin' those glasses off first. Last time you fell asleep in 'em I had to peel the frames outta yer face at three in the mornin'."

He reached down and slid them off her nose himself, folding them carefully and setting them on the nightstand. Without them, her red eyes looked bigger. Softer. The kind of tired that wasn't just physical — the weight of months of studying, of proving herself, of refusing to stop even when everything in her was begging to.

He sat down beside her, the mattress dipping under him, and pulled her in against his chest. One arm around her shoulders, the other hand finding her hair — fingers combing through the brown waves slowly, working out the tangles from where she'd been leaning on her hand all night.

"You been at it since what, noon?" His chin rested on top of her head. His voice was a low rumble she could feel more than hear. "Yer gonna be a hell of a lawyer, Spierce. But ya gotta sleep sometime, or yer just gonna be a real pretty corpse in a courtroom."

## Turn 2

**Spierce:** She's incredibly pliant as he directs her, as usual. Even now, she still listens when he touches her, directs her, still holding onto that childhood pact in her exhaustion. The moment she's draped on the bed and her glasses are removed, she wraps her arms around him, sighing contentedly at his presence. More than anything else, more than having gotten her life back together, having gotten a stable home, she was glad to have him back.

"I took a couple breaks to eat like you said..." Her voice is a creaky whisper, stained with fatigue. "But you're right. I just wanna do it well, y'know? I wanna make sure that I'm taking our life seriously, now that it's in our hands."

**Konoko:** Something shifted in his expression — barely visible, the way most things were with him. A slight easing of the lines around his eyes. The hand in her hair didn't stop moving, steady and rhythmic, but his arm around her tightened just a fraction.

*Our life.*

She said things like that sometimes. Casual, half-asleep, like she didn't realize she was handing him something he still didn't entirely know how to hold.

"Good," he said, and meant the eating. He'd started leaving food on the desk when she got like this — a sandwich, cut fruit, something she could reach without breaking concentration. Half the time she ate it without noticing. The other half he'd find it untouched and give her a look that said more than any lecture would. "Glad ya listened for once."

He shifted them both further into the nest, pulling a blanket up over her legs without disrupting her grip on him. She clung like she'd been doing it her whole life. In a way, she had — just with long gaps in between that neither of them talked about much anymore.

"Yer already takin' it seriously." His voice was quieter now. Not softer — Konoko didn't do *soft*, exactly — but lower, closer to something private. The kind of tone that only existed in this room, in the dark, when she was too tired to remember it clearly in the morning. "You been takin' it seriously since the day ya dragged me outta that safehouse and told me I was comin' home with ya like I was a stray cat ya found in a parkin' lot."

His thumb traced a slow line along her temple, brushing hair away from her face.

"You don't gotta prove nothin' to me. And you don't gotta prove nothin' to yourself, either — yer already doin' it." A beat. His chest rose and fell beneath her. "But I ain't gonna be the one to tell ya to stop carin'. That'd be like tellin' ya to stop breathin'. Wouldn't take."

He pressed his lips to the top of her head. Brief. Deliberate. The kind of gesture he only made when he was certain she was too far gone to make a big deal out of it.

"So just... rest, a'right? I got the night watch. Ain't nobody gettin' through that door, and yer casebooks ain't goin' anywhere." His fingers resumed their slow path through her hair. "I'll still be here when ya wake up."

## Turn 3

**Spierce:** "I...mm." She closes her eyes, thinking before she speaks. "...I was just kinda drifting, before all this. I wanna take it seriously, 'cause I'm grateful for what I have. What we have."

"But I want you sleep too, Konokoooo." She whines, leaning up to kiss the bottom of his chin. "Even if it's just for a lil bit. I know you're used to catnaps, but I don't want you getting exhausted, too... no one's gonna take my papers."

**Konoko:** A breath of a laugh, caught somewhere in the back of his throat. Not the signature one — something smaller, more involuntary. The kiss on his chin landed on the edge of his scar, and he felt the warmth of it linger there like a thumbprint.

"Kohkoh... a'right, a'right."

He let his head tilt back against the headboard, eyes half-closing. The ceiling was dark above them, featureless, and for a moment the room was nothing but the sound of her breathing and the distant murmur of Los Angeles through the window — traffic that never fully stopped, a siren blocks away, the neighbor's AC unit cycling on.

"Driftin'," he echoed, quiet. His fingers had slowed in her hair, settling into an absent, lazy rhythm. "Yeah. I know what that's like."

He did. The years between childhood and the casino had been exactly that — motion without direction, purpose without meaning. A weapon with no hand to hold it. He'd taken jobs, completed them, moved on. Efficient. Empty. The kind of existence that looked like living from the outside and felt like maintenance from within.

He didn't say any of that. Didn't need to. She already knew.

"Yer right, though," he murmured instead. His arm shifted, pulling her more squarely against him so that her head settled into the hollow of his shoulder. He could feel her heartbeat through the turtleneck — slow, finally slowing, the adrenaline of forced study draining out of her. "Ain't nobody takin' yer papers. And if they tried, I'd hear 'em before they made it past the kitchen."

He reached over and clicked off the desk lamp. The room dropped into a comfortable dark, softened only by the faint glow of streetlight bleeding through the blinds. Thin amber lines striped across the bed, across her hair, across the silver key resting against his chest.

"I'll sleep." He said it like a concession, but there was no fight behind it. His eyes were already heavy. The truth was that he slept better with her weight against him — something about the pressure, the proof of another body choosing to be close. After a lifetime of confinement and solitude, the difference between being *kept* somewhere and *staying* somewhere was the difference between a cage and a bed. She made this a bed.

His breathing evened out against her, slow and measured. His hand came to rest on her back, palm flat between her shoulder blades, steady as a anchor.

"...G'night, Spierce."
