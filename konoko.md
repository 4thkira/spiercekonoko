Its family name is kute Lonpanpa, and is known and feared by many in the world's dark underbelly. Kute, a noun meaning the ear or a verb meaning to listen or obey, functions in place of kulupu, the word for "group" or "community." They're of the belief that the meaning behind this change goes without saying.

Meanwhile, Lonpanpa is a fusion of three roots: lon (to exist), lanpan (to seize), and anpa (to bring low). To seize existence and drag it beneath.

Konoko's true name is kept private: a secret from any who may use it against him. In its place, it uses ilo Konoko. Ilo means “tool”, taking the place of jan, the word for “person.” Konoko is a compound: kon (hidden reality, unseen) and oko (eye, to see). Simply, a tool that sees what cannot be seen. The way Konoko himself typically translates it, however, is an instrument of the invisible eye.

Altogether, its name is ilo Konoko kute Lonpanpa, or "a seeing tool that obeys the command that ends lives."

Rather than give itself a person's name, the title of a tool felt more necessary—especially when personhood was already assumed. Humanity was too obvious. A lack of humanity needed to be said outloud.
